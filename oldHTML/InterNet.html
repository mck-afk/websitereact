Books

The best part of reading these is knowing that not a single word was written by AI.

    Frankenstein or, The Modern Prometheus by Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley 1818
    as relevant today as it was when it was written.
    Parable of The Sower by Octavia E. Butler 1993
    set in 2024-2027, Olamina leads with compassion while a president called Donner destroys the country.
    Caliban and the Witch by Slivia Federici 2004
    capitalism has always exploited womens labour and Motherwork.
    The Hunger Games (all of them!) by Suzanne Collins 2008-
    art imitates life, and this series has it all: just war politics, social contracts, propaganda.
    Capitalist Realism by Mark Fisher 2009
    "capitalism has colonized the dreaming life of the population".
    Circe by Madeline Miller 2018
    there is so much power lying in wait in the commitment to your craft.
    Chain-Gang All-Stars by Nana Kwame Adjei-Brenyah 2023
    powerful commentary on the sickening spectacle that is violence.
    Come Together by Emily Nagoski 2024
    practical and emotional guidance on reclaiming pleasure.
    The Serviceberry by Robin Wall Kimmerer 2024
    short, sweet, and packs a potent punch. hope-full.
